# Bond Return Swap (BRS)

> **Scope:** Generic product definition — system-agnostic. No Murex, Calypso, or BOX specifics.  
> **Layer:** Qaracter — reusable across all programmes and client environments.  
> **Purpose:** Reference for any team (accounting, development, AI agents) needing to understand what a BRS is and how it works economically.

---

## 1. What is a Bond Return Swap?

A Bond Return Swap (BRS) is a bilateral contract in which:

- **One party (Performance Buyer / Protection Seller)** receives the **total return** of an underlying bond — price appreciation/depreciation plus any coupon income generated by the bond during the contract period.
- **The other party (Performance Seller / Protection Buyer)** pays that total return and receives in exchange a **financing rate** — either fixed or floating (e.g. SOFR, EURIBOR, SELIC).

The total return of the bond includes both:
- **Income component:** coupons paid by the underlying bond during the life of the contract
- **Price component:** the change in the bond's market value between two fixing dates (positive if the price rises, negative if it falls)

If the bond price decreases over a period, the Performance Buyer pays the loss of value in addition to the financing rate.

BRS is classified as a **Security Finance** product — it is primarily used to manage bond inventories and exposures.

---

## 2. Economic Purpose

| Perspective | Party | Use Case |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| **Performance Seller** (pays total return, receives financing rate) | Hedged party — typically a bond holder | Reduces market exposure on a bond position without selling it. Hedges against price and credit fluctuation. |
| **Performance Buyer** (receives total return, pays financing rate) | Leveraged party | Gains full economic exposure to the bond without purchasing it. Useful for accessing markets with restricted access, avoiding settlement/custody complexity, or for leveraged positioning. |

**Key point:** The Performance Seller does not need to own the underlying bond. The BRS is most often a pure cash flow agreement indexed on the bond's performance — no physical delivery of the bond is required (unless specifically agreed).

---

## 3. Structure: Two Legs

Every BRS has two legs:

### Performance Leg (Asset Leg)
- Delivers the **total return** of the underlying bond
- Computed as: coupon flows + (end price − start price) × notional
- The price used (clean or dirty) is set at contract level
- The leg is sensitive to the bond's market price, credit spread, and any coupon payments

### Financing Leg (Interest Leg)
- Pays or receives a **financing rate** on the notional
- Typically: floating rate (SOFR, EURIBOR, SELIC, etc.) ± spread, or a fixed rate
- Calculated using a day count convention (e.g. ACT/360, BUS/252)
- Notional can be fixed or mark-to-market (reset to bond's current market value)

## 4. Types of BRS

### 4.1 Standard BRS (Single Currency)
Performance and financing leg in the same currency as the underlying bond. The simplest and most common structure.

### 4.2 Cross-Currency BRS

BRS supports four cross-currency structures:

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Full Composite** | Performance and financing leg both in a different currency from the bond. Coupons converted to the payment currency. | Bond in EUR; performance and financing leg in USD |
| **Full Composite with coupons in bond currency** | Performance and financing in a different currency, but coupons paid in the bond's own currency. | Bond in EUR; performance in USD; coupons in EUR; financing in USD |
| **Half Composite** | Performance and financing legs in different currencies. | Bond in EUR; performance leg in EUR; financing leg in USD |
| **Non-Deliverable (NDTRS)** | Performance and financing flows computed in the bond currency, then converted to a payment currency at settlement. Unlike composite BRS, the performance is computed in bond currency before conversion. | Bond in BRL; flows computed in BRL; paid in USD |

FX Delta sensitivity is supported for all cross-currency types.

### 4.3 BRS on Amortized Bond
Amortization is configured at bond definition level — no additional BRS-level configuration is needed.

For an **amortized bond**, the face value is paid down gradually with each coupon. At time *t*, the amortization factor **AF(t)** represents the percentage of face value still outstanding. The remaining premium is:

```
Premium(t) = (Price(t) - Face Value) × AF(t)
```

Between coupon payments, AF(t) is constant.

### 4.4 BRS on Inflation Bond
Inflation indexation is configured at bond definition level — no additional BRS-level configuration is needed.

An inflation bond has prices and coupons indexed to an inflation rate. The BRS behaves identically to a standard BRS, with the bond's inflation-adjusted price feeding the performance calculation.

### 4.5 BRS on Convertible Bond
No additional BRS-level configuration is needed.

A convertible bond is a bond with an option to convert into equity. The BRS behaves normally, except that the bond price is indexed on the stock price. Equity Greeks (delta, gamma) can be derived.

## 5. Key Economic Concepts

### Price Fixing
At each fixing date, the bond's current market price is observed and compared to the previous fixing price. The difference × notional = the performance payment. Fixings can be:
- **Periodic:** at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly) during the life of the swap
- **At maturity:** a single fixing at contract end (bullet structure, single period)

### Price Reference Type
The price used for the performance calculation — **clean price** or **dirty price** — is agreed at trade level. This determines whether accrued coupon is included in the return calculation.

### Coupon Pass-Through
Coupons paid by the underlying bond during the life of the contract are passed through to the Performance Buyer. The timing and currency of coupon pass-through payments are configured per trade (e.g. upon receipt, on the performance payment date, at maturity).

### Notional
The notional of the financing leg can be:
- **Fixed (Based on Initial Price):** constant throughout the life of the swap
- **Mark-to-Market:** reset to the current market value of the bond at each fixing date
- **Haircut-adjusted:** notional = market value × (1 − haircut%)

---

## 6. Cash Flow Summary

| Cash Flow | Direction | Trigger |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Performance payment | Buyer → Seller (if bond price fell) or Seller → Buyer (if bond price rose) | Each fixing date |
| Coupon pass-through | Seller → Buyer | When underlying bond pays coupon |
| Financing interest | Buyer → Seller (floating rate on notional) | Per financing leg schedule |
| Net settlement | Net of the above, or gross depending on agreement | Per payment schedule |

---

## 7. Risk Profile

| Risk | Performance Buyer | Performance Seller |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| Market risk (price) | Full exposure | Hedged |
| Credit risk (issuer default) | Exposed (bond price falls to zero) | Protected (receives compensation) |
| Interest rate risk | Partial (via financing leg) | Partial |
| FX risk (cross-currency BRS) | Exposed if not hedged | Exposed if not hedged |
| Counterparty risk | Both parties — mitigated by CSA/collateral | Both parties |
